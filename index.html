This handbook is your go-to reference for how we operate, what we expect of one another, and how we bring our values to life every single day. 🚌 Arrival & Dismissal

  • All staff help monitor student arrival daily.
  • Teachers greet students at classroom doors every day — on time and ready.
  • All staff must be at duty stations or outside classrooms promptly.
  • All dismissal requests come through the main office — redirect any adult requesting a student directly from you to the front office to sign them out.
  • Each homeroom teacher walks their class outside to buses/car pickup at the designated time.
  • Goal: buses leave by 2:45 pm. Remain in the building until your last student is headed home.
  • Front office or nurse will notify you if a student is sent home during lunch or recess.
⚠️
Dismissal Changes: Notify the office immediately if you receive any info about a change to a student's dismissal. Address these first thing in the morning — forward any family emails. Teachers will be notified of end-of-day changes via the dismissal printout.
🌧️
Early Dismissal Days: Supervise your students until they've all left. Students follow their normal plan unless a family emails [email protected] with a change. If you receive a family message via Remind/Text, notify the office as well.

📋 Student Attendance

  • Submit attendance electronically in PowerSchool by 8:30 am daily.
  • Students arriving after 8:05 am are considered tardy and need a slip from the office.
  • Submit office folders (notes, dismissal changes, forms) to the front office by 9:00 am.
  • The office will follow up with families to verify absences.

👁️ Student Supervision

🔴
Student supervision is our top priority. Never leave students unattended. If you need to step out, arrange coverage with a neighboring teacher or call the front office at X25000.
  • If you have a specific duty (car duty, lunch duty, etc.), you are responsible for finding your own coverage if you can't make it on time.
  • Teachers are responsible for picking classes up from end-of-day Specials and walking them outside to the buses.

🏛️ Building Care

We have the newest school in the region and are committed to treating it with the respect and care our community deserves.

Teachers & TAs:

  • Help students keep classrooms and common areas clean and orderly.
  • Keep doors locked at all times — no magnets in exterior doors. Close any propped door immediately.
  • Close windows and blinds, turn off lights when leaving for the day.
  • Keep classroom door window glass uncovered to the hallway (safety requirement).
  • Clean up shared workrooms/equipment after use.
  • Use painter's tape only when hanging displays — duct tape and packaging tape damage walls.
  • Maintain any hallway displays your class created.

Students:

  • Clean out cubby/personal space at end of each day.
  • Put away instructional materials when an activity is finished.
  • Stack or push in chairs at end of day.
🔧
Need something fixed? Submit a work order through the SharePoint site.

🔑 Building Access

Each teacher is issued a classroom key and a name badge that opens the front door.

🔒
After-hours access: If entering before 6:45 am, after 9:45 pm, or on weekends/holidays when custodians aren't present, you'll need an alarm code. See Crystal to get set up. If you trigger the alarm without a code, wait in the main parking lot for police to arrive with your school ID — do not leave. Notify Seth afterward.

🐾 Animals in School

Animals may not visit classrooms without a vet/pet store certification that they are disease-free, per Board Policy KKB ↗.

  • Check for student allergies before any animal visit.
  • Obtain written permission ↗ from every parent in your class.
  • Clear all animal visits with the principal in advance.

🚨 Emergency Situations

🚨
In an actual crisis: The adult with the injured person should (1) notify the office and (2) call 911. Do not wait — dispatchers will have questions that require you to stay on the line.

Media/Communication rule: All staff must refer any inquiries from the press, parents, or community members to school administration during any emergency. Do not communicate directly — this can cause confusion and panic.

📁 Each Teacher Should Always Have:

  • Safety drill procedures (fire, ALICE, tornado, earthquake)
  • Updated class lists with parent contact information and student allergies
  • A substitute folder containing the Crisis Plan and a list of students with medical conditions or SPED needs

🔥 Fire Drills

  • Typically weekly in August/September, monthly thereafter.
  • Know your classroom fire exit plan AND the plan for common areas (cafeteria, gym, music room, etc.).
  • Bring class lists and keys, escort students to the designated exterior area.
  • Take attendance at the exterior location and report any missing students to administration immediately.
  • Do not return to the building until the all-clear is given.

🔒 Lockdown (ALICE)

📋
In a lockdown, follow your ALICE training protocols. Three quick references are always available to you:

1. Your ALICE quick reference guide posted by your classroom door
2. The county training guide on the back of your staff ID badge
3. Your ALICE training — if you need a refresher, contact administration
  • Trust your training. ALICE gives you options — Alert, Lockdown, Inform, Counter, Evacuate — use your judgment based on the situation.
  • Keep students calm and yourself calm. Your demeanor sets the tone.
  • Do not open the door for anyone until an all-clear comes from administration.

🌪️ Natural Disasters

In the event of a tornado or earthquake, advise all students and staff to take shelter in the hallway — seated quietly, heads bowed, arms over their heads.

🏃 Building Evacuation

  • Fire alarm or "evacuate" call = get students out and as far from the building as possible.
  • During a weather emergency, bring students to the main building if time allows.
  • Families cannot pick up children directly from learning cottages — direct them to the main office.

Professional Expectations

📜 ACPS Standards of Conduct

All staff are expected to uphold the ACPS Standards of Conduct (Policy GBC) ↗ — these define our professional baseline as employees of the division.

⏰ Contract Hours

Per Virginia State Code (8VAC20-490-30), "Contract Hours" include time inside the building during the day, but staff responsibilities extend beyond those hours as needed during the school year.

  • Be ready to welcome students in doorways before the 7:50 am bell.
  • If an emergency prevents you from being at your location at 7:50 am, notify the main office and your partner teacher right away.
  • On delayed openings, you may report late by the amount of the delay (2-hour delay = report by 9:40 am).
  • If you need to leave campus during the school day for any reason, notify front office staff when you leave and when you return.
📋
Part-time teacher, TA, and cafeteria worker schedules are set by school administration and cannot be adjusted without prior written approval.

👔 Dress Code

We represent Mountain View in everything we do. The Board requires employees to conduct themselves — and dress — in a manner that reflects well on us as a school division. Our expectation: casual workplace attire, or simply: "Clean & covered."

📧 Email Etiquette

Staff email is our primary channel for routine communications — Staff Announcements, meeting agendas, daily logistics. These emails are expected to be read, as they reduce the need for additional meetings.

  • Before hitting send, ask: Is this appropriate? Is it clear? Would I be comfortable if it were public?

Instructional Framework

🌱 Responsive Classroom

We strive to create a safe, caring, and engaging learning environment where all students feel welcome and inspired to learn. The Responsive Classroom approach guides how we show up for kids each day.

"The social curriculum is as important as the academic curriculum. How children learn is as important as what they learn." Responsive Classroom Core Principle

The 7 Core Principles:

  • The social curriculum is as important as the academic curriculum.
  • How children learn is as important as what they learn — process and content go hand in hand.
  • The greatest cognitive growth occurs through social interaction.
  • Children need a core set of social skills to succeed: C.A.R.E.S.
  • Knowing the children we teach — individually, culturally, and developmentally — is as important as knowing content.
  • Knowing students' families and working with them as partners is essential.
  • How adults work together is as important as individual competence. Lasting change begins with the adult community.

C.A.R.E.S. Framework

C

A

Teacher Best Practices in RC include:

  • Morning Meeting — gathering as a whole class to greet, share, and warm up for the day
  • Rule Creation — co-creating classroom rules with students tied to community goals
  • Interactive Modeling — teaching expected behaviors through a structured modeling technique
  • Positive Teacher Language — using words and tone that promote active learning and self-discipline
  • Logical Consequences — responding to misbehavior in ways that allow kids to fix and learn from mistakes while preserving dignity
  • Guided Discovery — introducing materials with formats that spark creativity and responsibility
  • Academic Choice — increasing motivation through teacher-structured student choices in work
  • Classroom Organization — setting up physical space for independence, cooperation, and productivity
  • Working with Families — sharing insights and helping families understand your approach
  • Collaborative Problem Solving — conferencing, role-playing, and student-led problem-solving
🤫
Most teachers use a chime followed by a raised hand as the quiet signal. When an adult raises their hand, students (and other adults) know to become quiet and ready for instructions.

📋 Classroom Rules

  • Classroom rules are co-created with students — their input and participation matters.
  • Teachers model the rules; students practice them.
  • Rules are posted visibly in the classroom.
  • Rules are communicated to families.

🌍 Culturally Responsive Education

1
Culturally Responsive Teachers acknowledge and incorporate students' cultural heritage — and reflect on their own.
Create welcoming classrooms. Recognize your own cultural influences and how they shape your communication, expectations, and teaching. Teach to and through student backgrounds. Explore the cultures your students bring.
2
Culturally Responsive Teachers plan instruction that is differentiated, rigorous, and relevant.
Use varied instructional strategies that reflect multiculturalism. Plan lessons that capitalize on student cultures and experiences. Provide student choice and present content in varied modalities to reach multiple intelligences.
3
Culturally Responsive Teachers build positive learning partnerships with students and families.
Build relationships that enhance student achievement. Make meaningful connections around culture, interests, and learning styles. Recognize individual strengths. Make parents true partners in their child's education.

💻 Technology

Teachers are responsible for knowing and applying Albemarle County's Acceptable Use of Technology Policy ↗ — for students and themselves.

Technology is a tool for facilitating and enhancing instruction, not a replacement for it. Every student should have meaningful opportunities to use technology to access the curriculum in powerful ways.

🗣️ Teaching About Controversial Issues

Per School Board Policy INB, when covering controversial topics, multiple perspectives must be presented so students learn to think critically and form their own informed views. When providing sources, students have the right to access materials containing a wide range of viewpoints to develop critical reading and thinking skills.

🤲 School Based Intervention Team (SBIT)

When you have concerns about a student's academic or behavioral needs, begin by implementing interventions and tracking progress in your classroom. When you need team support, use the SBIT request form on the staff portal to request an initial meeting.

📑 IEPs & 504 Plans

⚖️
IEPs are legal contracts. Teachers are legally required to know and implement all IEP and 504 accommodations for their students — starting on Day 1. Failure to provide accommodations exposes the school to legal action. Questions? See the student's case manager.

Section V

Family & Community Engagement

👨‍👩‍👧 Parent/Teacher Conferences

Learning Partnerships between home and school is CRT Lens #3. Formal conferences are a key opportunity to build those connections. Conferences can be held in person or via Zoom and serve to:

  • Open two-way communication with families
  • Become acquainted with families and their funds of knowledge
  • Report student progress
  • Answer parent questions and hear concerns
🤝
For students receiving ESOL, SpEd, or intervention services — coordinate conferences with those teachers. These conferences should follow the scheduled days unless that doesn't work for the family. The ESOL team arranges interpreters for families during conference time.

🎉 Classroom Celebrations

We honor the diversity of our students and families. We're sensitive to the fact that not everyone celebrates in the same way — and we're committed to consistency across classrooms at each grade level.

  • Be consistent across your grade level — coordinate with your team.
  • Let the office know the date, time, and location of any activities.
  • Ensure all celebrations are culturally responsive and inclusive of all students in your class.
  • No food should be used for classroom celebrations.

🎂 Student Birthdays

  • Each grade level decides how birthday students are honored (hat, certificate, etc.) — share your approach!
  • Limit birthday recognition to students in your own class only.
  • We discourage food (cupcakes, etc.) for birthdays due to allergies.
  • Birthday invitations may only be distributed at school if every student in the class is invited. No selective distribution.

🪪 Visitors & Volunteers

Visitors must check in and out through the front office and wear a visitor's badge at all times. It is every staff member's responsibility to approach any unfamiliar adult not wearing a badge: a simple "May I help you find someone?" and a prompt to visit the office is appropriate.

👀
Even visitors you personally know may be unfamiliar to others in the hall or cafeteria. It takes all of us to keep everyone safe.

We welcome and encourage family and community volunteers. All volunteers must complete the Volunteer Registration process before volunteering or chaperoning. Direct questions to Pam or Crystal.

🌐 Interpretation & Translation

Families have the right to receive communication in their home language. All documents sent to Mountain View families must be translated into Spanish.

To find a family's home language, click the backpack in the PowerTeacher portal → select the student → from the Select Screens menu choose ESOL Student Data.
